Romanian Classes

Romanian Language, Culture, and Civilization Course

Presentation of the course

Through the efforts of the Embassy of Romania in Dublin beginning from 2015 the Romanian Language, Culture, and Civilization course is tought in some of the schools of Dublin. It started with one school, Hartstown Community School and it extended from year to year and at the moment is taking place in six school and is still extending. The course had a very great success and more and more parents want to enroll their children for the Romanian Classes. The Romanian course is for pupil aged 4-18, and within the course there is preparation for the Leaving Certificate examination for the graduate year students. The possibility to chose Romanian as a subject for an exam with such importance is a great advantage for native Romanian students. The marks of this subjects add up directly to the final, resulting in a higher mark and increasing the possibility to be admitted to the desired college. he Romanian Language exam is a higher-level exam, but this should not discourage anyone to chose it, as it’s not supposed to be an extremely difficult one for any Romanian speaker. Still some preparation is needed, especially in the speciality area.

The course is managed and financed by the Romanian Language Institute in Buckarest and takes places in several other European countries where there are significant number of Romanians: Italy, Spain, Belgium, Portugal, and Irlanda. The course is free both for the school and parents, all costs are covered by the Romanian Language Institute, and taught by a calified teacher, selected by the Institute.

The the pupils in the primary school, learn the language through playing, songs and poems. They learn to write, to read, to discover the wonderful world of tales and books. After they are able to write, pupil are encouraged to read and get to know authors and writings from the Romanian literature, information about Romania's history and geography, and also about some customs and tradiotions. Through all of this we want to form their own national identity and help them find their own origin and identity.

The secondary school students are learning to write correctly in Romanian, they are encouraged to communicate and to read in their native language. They are learning about the most imoportant Romanian literature and authors, important aspects of Romanian histoty and geoghrapghy, customs and traditions and so on. All adapted to their age.

The classes are tailored to be attractive and that each student would take part in each sequence of learning. The process of teaching is interactive and dinamic, there is dialog all along the classes and the pupils are stimulated to learn something new each time.
