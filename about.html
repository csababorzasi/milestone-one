Our Vision

Our aim is to make possible for every Romanian child in Dublin to be able to learn their native language. We cannot emphasize enough how important is for a child to learn and be able to use Romanaian as a native, natural language. Our aim is that our children will become bilingual, being able to speak Romanian as well as English

Because it is the first language a child learns, the home language plays a very important role in the development of the child’s brain and identity. The home language continues to be important as the child grows up, making it easier for them to learn other languages and school subjects in general. The bilingual student has a big advantage over those who only have one language. Bilingual students are generally stronger at problem-solving and find it easier to understand difficult concepts - in Maths for example.

Knowing your home language will help your child to:

  • Develop skills like observation, analysis, reflection, questioning, exploration – all very necessary in the learning process
  • Transfer these skills to learning not only languages, but other subjects like Maths and Science as well
  • Increase your child’s understanding of a topic and help them to learn English more quickly
  • Have confidence in who they are. Confidence is very important for success in learning and in life
  • Remain connected to her wider family, e.g., grandparents, cousins. They can communicate with confidence when using Skype and when visiting your country
  • Develop an appreciation for the culture and traditions of their culture of origin and feel at home in your country of origin
  • Be interested and motivated to learn
  • Develop independent/autonomous and lifelong learning skills

The world is becoming increasingly diverse. Languages have an important role to play. Knowing more than on language

  • enriches a person’s knowledge and understanding of the world, other cultures and people
  • helps to broaden and enrich personal contacts
  • builds bridges between people of different cultures
  • helps us to understand each other, work together and build a more harmonious society

Languages are increasingly important for career success. “They are critically important to Ireland’s trade and potential to trade” (Department of Business, Enterprise and Innovation).

There are greater advantages in the jobs market for graduates who:

  • have an extra language
  • are flexible in the way they use their language skills
  • can use the languages they know in different contexts and for different purposes
